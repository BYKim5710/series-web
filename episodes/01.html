Author Introduction

Byung Youn Kim

Born in 1957, Byung Youn Kim graduated from the Department of Business Administration at Yonsei University and completed the MBA program at Purdue University. He worked for more than 20 years in Daewoo Shipbuilding and Samsung Electronics, primarily in marketing. He also served as the Head of Internationalization and Foreign Language Education at Samsung Human Resources Development Center. Later, he worked as an adjunct professor and lecturer at Gachon University, Shingu University, Yonsei University (Wonju Campus), and Howon University. Currently, he is running Dures Management Research Institute, a company he founded.

His published works include The Samsung Myth Has Yet a Long Way to Go, The Gordian Knot, Opening Pandora's Box of Korea, Thirty Letters to Jeong-Ah, and Soul and Zero (ÈùàÍ≥º Èõ∂).

The Beginning: Fear of Death and a 1977 Experience

When he was in the 5th grade, the fear of death first struck him deeply as he witnessed the passing of his great-grandparents. Since then, the fear of death continued to torment him. In the spring of 1977, as he crossed the university gate, he had a life-altering experience akin to being struck by lightning on a clear day. He rushed into the office of KUSA (Korean UNESCO Student Association) in a dazed state and scribbled down something while in a semi-conscious state.

‚ÄúAll beings consist of body, mind, and spirit. The body and mind, which experience fatigue, will perish. But the spirit, which has existed since the beginning and will exist eternally, is the essence of all beings. This spirit never dies and continues to exist.‚Äù

Expanding the Inquiry: Scriptures and Philosophy

To find answers, he began immersing himself in religious texts and encountered various scriptures. He also grew curious about how philosophers approached these issues and delved into their writings.
